MEMORY &DIGNITY&ACTION

there are multiple kinds of ‘political memory’ projects for the struggle against cop city happening already. one is the Day of the Forest Defender, whose meaning is deepened with action and gathering every January 18th. another is a repository of ephemera in the southwest, now digitized, which builds resiliency to suppression and seizure of our shit.

to use something is to preserve it: the basic understanding which connects these disparate efforts is that we can study ourselves, and we can produce the knowledge as living culture, agit-prop, and material changes to the world.

in so-called new york we’re making a public exhibition with events with elders and students, print nights training on a risograph, structured research time, etc. it’s made to be reproducible and expand - we hope that you can add to its complexity and breadth with your submissions, use it to establish organizational links, study nights, and the will to fight wherever you are.

for life and against cop cities, city halls, and hollywoods.
